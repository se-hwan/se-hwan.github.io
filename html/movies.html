Movies

Or "Films"

I really don't watch a lot of TV shows, but there are a few sprinkled in here too.
Someone I know accused me several times of getting too excited while describing a movie and spoiling it completely. I'll try my best to avoid that here.

Favorites:

My favorite movies and shows. Might not be for everyone.
  • Whiplash - 2014
  • La La Land - 2016
    • I've watched this too many times. I love how Damien Chazelle directs his movies.
  • 500 Days of Summer - 2009
    • This starts out like a pretty cliche romcom, and it stays that way for a good while, and also arguably ends that way, but I still love this movie. It made me think about relationships in a different light.
  • The Secret Life of Walter Mitty - 2013
    • "To see the world, things dangerous to come to, to see behind walls, draw closer, to find each other, and to feel. That is the purpose of life."
    • I love the cinematography in this. It is so artistic and deliberate.
  • Up in the Air - 2009
  • Good Will Hunting - 1997
    • It's hard for me to choose any particular scene for this because all of it is just so good.
    • But here's one..
    • And another.
    • Just one more.
    • This movie's about so many things, but I like it the most because of how it shows what it means to be vulnerable to be someone else.
  • Inception - 2010
  • Avatar: The Last Airbender - 2014
    • For what was supposed to be a kid's show, it deals with war, sacrifice, love, and responsibility in such creative ways. I grew up with this show, so nostalgia definitely plays a factor in it being placed here, but I've rewatched it many, many times and I only appreciate it more.
    • Toph.
  • Bojack Horseman - 2014-2020
    • This show could easily make you depressed. I've never met a show that feels more real, and its full of talking animals. You'll either get weirded out by it, or think it's one of the greatest shows ever - I haven't met many people who have tried it in between.
  • Ratatouille - 2007
  • Gladiator - 2000
    • "What we do in life, echoes in eternity."
    • Shivers.
  • Groundhog Day - 1993
