Piano

I learned piano formally when I was around 10, and like many people, quit after learning the scales and a few pieces. I don't think I ever disliked playing - I just hated how I was learning it. After hearing a friend of mine play in a practice room in high school however, I began practicing again through my senior year of high school and into college. Unfortunately, my knowledge of music theory has become very choppy and informal as a result (and I'd learned all the terms in Korean, which didn't help).

I feel like the piano is especially well designed, not only from an engineering standpoint but also as an instrument in itself. Like the best games or sports, it is easy to pick up and nearly impossible to fully master, and people can, and have been, endlessly creative with what can be done with it.

While I'll probably never be as skilled as those truly dedicated to it, I still want to learn to play the songs I enjoy most and more about music in general. To that end, I've organized the following for when I have more time (and hopefully one day, my own piano).

Guitar

My freshman year roommate (a computer science major who occasionally browses this website to mock my HTML skills) is the one who first introduced me to guitar. It's interesting just how different and similar it was to piano. The keyboard was instead a fretboard, and volume was controlled by plucking rather than pressing. I remember the first few weeks of learning made my fingertips completely blistered. Since learning the basics of chords and fingerstyle, I've more recently moved on to the electric guitar - rock has always been my favorite genre of music and I'd want to be able to play the iconic solos I've heard so often myself. That being said, I have a lot more music theory and basic skills to brush up on before I'll be able to.

Violin

I've only recently begun to become interested in violin (and the cello, to some extent), and I wish it had caught my attention sooner. It definitely has the highest learning curve of the three here, but its sound is so unique and different in tone from what I'm used to when playing piano. For example, Married Life from the soundtrack of the Pixar movie Up would feel completely different if it had been played on piano instead of violin. I have no idea when I'll be able to afford or learn the violin on my own (I don't think it would be wise to self-teach in this case), but I hope I will some day. I don't have much exposure to classical music, but I'm beginning to really enjoy it.
