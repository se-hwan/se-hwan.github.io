Books

Favorites:

Some of my favorite books. I strongly recommend each and every one of these.
  • Good Omens: The Nice and Accurate Prophecies of Agnes Nutter, Witch - Neil Gaiman and Terry Pratchett
    • "It is said that the Devil has all the best tunes. This is broadly true. But Heaven has the best choreographers"
    • "Aziraphale. The Enemy, of course. But an enemy for six thousand years now, which made him a sort of friend."
    • "“Heaven has no taste."
      "Now-"
      "And not one single sushi restaurant."
      A look of pain crossed the angel's suddenly very serious face.”"
    • "Crowley (An Angel who did not so much Fall as Saunter Vaguely Downwards)"
    • Gaiman and Pratchett are incredible separately - together, they've made one of the funniest and most original stories I've read.
  • The Three Body Problem - Cixin Liu
  • Dune - Frank Herbert
    • The original Star Wars. A faithful, modern adaptation of the Bene Gesserit, ornithopters, and sandworms would be amazing.
  • American Gods - Neil Gaiman
    • "“Hey," said Shadow. "Huginn or Muninn, or whoever you are."
      The bird turned, head tipped, suspiciously, on one side, and it stared at him with bright eyes.
      "Say 'Nevermore,'" said Shadow.
      "Fuck you," said the raven.”"
  • Calvin and Hobbes - Bill Watterson
  • Life of a Colossus - Adrian Goldsworthy
    • The most exciting non-fiction book I've read. Made me completely obsessed with learning about Roman history during the fall of the Republic and rise of the Empire.
  • The Green Mile - Stephen King
    • "On the day of my judgment, when I stand before God, and He asks me why did I kill one of his true miracles, what am I gonna say? That it was my job? My job?"
    • "We each owe a death, there are no exceptions, I know that, but sometimes, oh God, the Green Mile is so long."
  • The Picture of Dorian Gray - Oscar Wilde
  • The Invention of Morel - Adolfo Bioy Casares
    • One of the most original stories I've read.
  • The Power of Habit - Charles Duhigg

An Inexhaustive List of Great Books I've Read:

In no particular order
  • His Dark Materials - Phillip Pullman
    • I read these when I was too young. Need to reread them at some point.
  • The ABC Murders - Agatha Christie
  • And Then There Were None - Agatha Christie
    • "One little Indian boy left all alone; He went and hanged himself and then there were none."
    • "It had come about ex­act­ly in the way things hap­pened in books."
    • Probably the best mystery I've read.
  • The Complete Works of Sherlock Holmes - Arthur Conan Doyle
    • But Poirot doesn't compare to Holmes.
    • While the Cumberbatch is fantastic as Sherlock, Mr. Holmes is an incredible, and underrated, take on the detective that I hope more people give a chance.
  • Masters of Command - Barry Strauss
    • Compares the generalships of Hannibal Barca, Alexander the Great, and Julius Caesar.
  • Siddartha - Herman Hesse
    • "I have always believed, and I still believe, that whatever good or bad fortune may come our way we can always give it meaning and transform it into something of value.""
    • "The river is everywhere."
  • Meditations - Marcus Aurelius
    • Surprisingly relatable for being almost 2000 years old. One passage describes the struggle of wanting to sleep in in the face of daily responsibilities. Reads like lessons from a wise father. Bonus points for being the Marcus Aurelius featured in one of the greatest movies ever.
  • The Messenger - Markus Zusak
  • The Book Thief - Markus Zusak
    • "A snowball in the face is surely the perfect beginning to a lasting friendship."
    • "It was a year for the ages, like 79, like 1346, to name just a few. Forget the scythe, Goddamn it, I needed a broom or a mop. And I needed a vacation."
    • This is one of the few books to bring me close to crying.
  • Mathematical Methods in Engineering - Joseph Powers and Mihir Sen
    • This also made me cry, in a very different way.
  • Slaughterhouse-Five - Kurt Vonnegut
    • "So it goes."
  • Invisible Cities - Italo Calvino
  • Butterball - Guy de Maupassant
  • The Prince - Niccolo Machiavelli
  • Man's Search for Meaning - Victor Frankl
  • The Alchemist - Paolo Coelho
  • The Harry Potter Series - J.K. Rowling
    • It's interesting how some people are known only by the initials for their names. I've read the Harry Potter books since I was ten, and not once did I ever learn what the J. or K. stood for. Still don't plan to.
  • The Hobbit - J.R.R. Tolkien
    • On that note, someone once told me the author's full name was Jolkien Rolkien Rolkien Tolkien, and I'm still happy believing that.
  • Different Seasons - Stephen King
    • Not many people know that Stephen King wrote the story that would become The Shawshank Redemption. He can write dramas as well as, if not better, than his horror in my opinion. I also just really hate his horror. I feel like King knows too well how people tick, and what terrifies them most.
  • The Fountainhead - Ayn Rand
  • Atlas Shrugged - Ayn Rand
  • Artemis Fowl - Eoin Colfer
  • Airman - Eoin Colfer
